Our History

Founded in 2009 in the aftermath of the devastating South Pacific Tsunami, the Global Disaster Relief Initiative began as a small volunteer group committed to helping affected communities in Samoa. Since then, we've grown into an international organization with a presence in 48 countries.

Over the past 15 years, we've responded to earthquakes, hurricanes, floods, wildfires, and humanitarian crises across six continents. Our growth has been driven by our commitment to communities and our evidence-based approach to disaster relief and recovery.

Our Approach

We believe in a three-phase approach to disaster management:

  • Immediate Response: Within 72 hours of a disaster, our rapid response teams deploy essential supplies, medical aid, and emergency shelter to affected areas.
  • Recovery Support: In the weeks and months following a disaster, we work with communities to restore access to clean water, rebuild infrastructure, and provide psychological support to survivors.
  • Resilience Building: Our long-term programs focus on disaster preparedness, community education, and sustainable rebuilding practices that reduce vulnerability to future disasters.

In 2024 alone, we've distributed over 1.8 million emergency kits, constructed 12,500 temporary shelters, and provided clean water to more than 850,000 people in disaster-affected regions.

Our Team

Behind our work is a dedicated team of 230 full-time staff and over 5,000 trained volunteers worldwide. Our team includes:

  • 65 Emergency Response Specialists
  • 42 Public Health Professionals
  • 28 Civil Engineers
  • 18 Psychologists specialized in trauma
  • 77 Regional Coordinators

Additionally, we partner with 140 local organizations across the globe to ensure our response is culturally sensitive and aligned with community needs.

Financial Transparency

We're committed to transparency and responsible stewardship of resources:

  • 87% of all funds go directly to program services
  • 9% to administrative costs
  • 4% to fundraising efforts

We've maintained a 4-star rating from Charity Navigator for 8 consecutive years and are accredited by the International Aid Transparency Initiative.
